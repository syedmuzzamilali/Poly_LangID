Quadrilingual Example Trace & Zero-Heuristic Design Situations

Below is the end-to-end formal execution of our Quadrilingual (English-Spanish-French-German) example through the 10-stage PolyBeta V5 pipeline, illustrating how every challenging code-switching situation is resolved mathematically without dictionaries or linguistic heuristics:

"This summer is wonderful, porque tenemos mucho sol en la playa, mais il fait un peu trop chaud ce soir, aber das Bier ist sehr kalt und lecker!"
Stage 1
Sentence Language Discovery (`SentenceLanguageDiscovery`)

Instead of forcing all 26 supported models (`polybeta.py`) to compete on every word (which introduces massive statistical confusion), Stage 1 discovers the exact active languages C = {'en', 'es', 'fr', 'de'} via three mechanisms:
• Global Sentence Prediction over the entire text via lid.176.ftz.
• Script-to-Candidate Anchoring (`SCRIPT_TO_LANGS`): Inspecting Unicode script classifications via offset-preserving tokenization so non-Latin scripts (`DEVANAGARI`, `ARABIC`, `HAN`, `BENGALI`, etc.) immediately anchor their target models (`hi`, `mr`, `ar`, `ur`, `zh`, etc.) even when touching characters without spaces.
• Multi-Scale Rolling Phrase Windows (1 to 6 words): Evaluating rolling windows down to 1 or 2 words (`dataset`, `Jangan kirim`) to ensure single loanwords and short switches enter the active language set without needing dictionaries.

Stage 2
Tokenization & SubToken Punctuation Alignment

The sentence is segmented into sub-tokens preserving exact character offsets and script classification (Latin). Crucially, trailing clause markers (playa,, soir,, lecker!) are mathematically bounded to their preceding word so punctuation never distorts character subword margins or splits boundaries.

Stage 3 & 4
Multi-Expert Evaluation & Boundary Analysis

Every token is evaluated by both FastText (subword character models) and Lingua (Markov chain n-gram tables), yielding probability distributions and confidence margins (the difference between the top two language probabilities). Unambiguous anchors (wonderful $\rightarrow$ en: 1.0, tenemos $\rightarrow$ es: 0.99, chaud $\rightarrow$ fr: 0.99, kalt $\rightarrow$ de: 1.0) establish immediate consensus peaks.

Stage 5 & 6
Evidence Validation & Local Context Activation

Situation Handled: Ambiguous Stopwords & Short Tokens. Short tokens (3 characters or less) or words with narrow confidence differences such as "is", "en", "il", and "ce" trigger local context activation. A 2-word phrase window is constructed around the token within the active language set. To prevent cross-clause contamination, window expansion strictly halts upon encountering clause punctuation (,, !, .) or script transitions (such as switching from Latin to Cyrillic). Thus, "il" and "ce" are evaluated cleanly inside "mais il fait" and "chaud ce soir", locking them to fr with high confidence.

Stage 7 & 8
Consensus Detection & Evidence Comparison

For each token, all evidence (Model Consensus, Local Context, and Raw Distortions) is compared via probability margins. Tokens where FastText and Lingua agree without boundary conflict lock directly into consensus without priority overrides.

Stage 9
Context Refinement (`ContextRefinement` / Zero-Heuristic Smoothing)

This stage resolves the three hardest boundary situations mathematically without hard-coded dictionary rules:

Situation A — Forward Clause Introduction (Orphan Conjunctions): When a short transition word (4 characters or less like "porque", "mais", "aber", or "e") sits directly between two distinct language blocks, syntactically it functions as a clause initiator. Unless the preceding language exhibits overwhelming probability superiority, PolyBeta projects the conjunction forward into the upcoming right-side clause: "porque" $\rightarrow \text{es}$, "mais" $\rightarrow \text{fr}$, "aber" $\rightarrow \text{de}$.

Situation B — Simultaneous Bridge Evaluation (`_refine_sandwiches`): Isolated single-word borrowings surrounded by identical neighboring languages inside the same script family are unified with the surrounding clause unless they strongly contradict that language.

Situation C — Differential Logit Attribution across Peaks: Between confident anchor peaks, probability differences assign low-confidence bridge words directly to the dominant surrounding peak.

Stage 10
Final Continuous Span Aggregation

Consecutive tokens sharing the exact same statistical label are merged into unified, continuous language tuples:
• ['This summer is wonderful, ' (EN)]
• ['porque tenemos mucho sol en la playa, ' (ES)]
• ['mais il fait un peu trop chaud ce soir, ' (FR)]
• ['aber das Bier ist sehr kalt und lecker!' (DE)]
