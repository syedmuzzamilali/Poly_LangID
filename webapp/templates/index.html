Quadrilingual Example Trace & Zero-Heuristic Design Situations

Below is a simple walkthrough of how our system processes a sentence containing four languages (English, Spanish, French, and German). It shows how the PolyBeta V5 pipeline easily separates and identifies each language:

"This summer is wonderful, porque tenemos mucho sol en la playa, mais il fait un peu trop chaud ce soir, aber das Bier ist sehr kalt und lecker!"
Stage 1
Discovering the Languages Present

Instead of guessing from all 26 supported languages on every single word (which causes confusion), the system first figures out exactly which languages are present in the whole sentence (English, Spanish, French, and German). It uses the FastText model to get a "big picture" view of the sentence and detects different alphabets (like Arabic or Chinese) automatically.

Stage 2
Splitting the Sentence into Words

The sentence is carefully split into individual words. Punctuation marks (like commas or exclamation points) are kept attached to their words so they don't cause mistakes during identification.

Stage 3 & 4
Evaluating Each Word using FastText and Lingua

Every single word is analyzed by two powerful AI models: FastText and Lingua. Very obvious words (like wonderful for English, or chaud for French) are immediately identified with high confidence.

Stage 5 & 6
Checking the Context for Short Words

Short words (like "is", "en", or "il") can belong to many languages and confuse the models. To fix this, the system looks at the surrounding words to gather "context". By looking at the words immediately next to them, it confidently locks these tricky short words to the correct language (for example, identifying that "il" belongs with the French phrase "mais il fait").

Stage 7 & 8
Bringing it All Together

The system compares the answers from both the FastText and Lingua models. When both models agree on the language of a word, that decision is locked in.

Stage 9
Fixing the Tricky Borders Between Languages

Sometimes a sentence switches languages right on a tiny transition word (like "porque" or "mais"). The system is smart enough to understand that these words usually introduce the next phrase, so it correctly groups "porque" with the Spanish section and "mais" with the French section. It also handles isolated "loanwords" by looking at the languages surrounding them.

Stage 10
Final Output

Finally, consecutive words of the same language are merged together into clean, continuous highlights:
• [This summer is wonderful, ] (English)
• [porque tenemos mucho sol en la playa, ] (Spanish)
• [mais il fait un peu trop chaud ce soir, ] (French)
• [aber das Bier ist sehr kalt und lecker!] (German)
